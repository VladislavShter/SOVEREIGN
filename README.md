  🎙️  SILENT SURVEILLANCE


Most users have heard the urban legend — "the phone listens to conversations and serves matching ads". Most technical specialists respond — "no, it doesn't listen, this is coincidence and targeting through other signals".

The truth is in between, and it is subtler. Direct mass surveillance of conversations through the microphone does not exist — it would be too expensive in traffic and technically noticeable. But there is something else:

   🎤  Many apps periodically activate the microphone for fractions of a second to capture an "acoustic fingerprint of surroundings" — to determine what room you are in, how noisy it is, whether there is a TV nearby and which channel is playing.

   📸  Many apps periodically take a snapshot with the front camera when unlocking the device or opening the app — officially "for biometric verification", in fact — for analysis of emotions, lighting, surroundings.

   👆  Some apps track tap frequency and pressure force — this is a new class of biometrics that allows identifying a person better than a fingerprint.

   📳  Any app with access to the accelerometer and gyroscope can reconstruct speech from micro-vibrations of the phone body — academic work has existed since 2014.

All this happens in the background. Quietly. Without explicit consent — or with consent hidden on page 47 of a user agreement that no one reads.

  ⚠️  𝐒𝐎𝐕𝐄𝐑𝐄𝐈𝐆𝐍 IS NOT A SANDBOX


This is important to understand before reading further.

There are dozens of apps on the market promising "privacy through cloning" — Parallel Space, Dual Apps, App Cloner, and similar. They all work on the same scheme — they create a virtual container inside their own process and run a "clone" of the app in it.

This is a sandbox. And it does not work.

Modern banking apps, government apps, and even many commercial apps know what a sandbox is and block themselves upon detecting one. They check:

   ▸  Is the process running with the correct Android user UID
   ▸  Does the APK signature match the expected one
   ▸  Is the system bootloader modified
   ▸  Are tools like Xposed or Magisk active
   ▸  Does the device pass Play Integrity / SafetyNet

Sandboxes fail on every one of these points. A banking app in Parallel Space will either not launch at all, or launch with limited functionality, or launch — and quietly inform the bank that it is running in a compromised environment. With consequences for your account.

Sovereign works differently. We use the standard system mechanisms of Android — the same ones that corporations use to separate "work" and "personal" on employees' devices. This is not a hack. This is the legal architecture built by Google and embedded in all modern Android. For apps living in this space, it is indistinguishable from a separate phone — because architecturally it is a separate space, with its own storage, its own encryption keys, its own network settings.

No hacking (no root). No system modification. No custom ROM. Installation is one button from the app store, like any other program.

       🌉  𝐒𝐎𝐕𝐄𝐑𝐄𝐈𝐆𝐍 𝐈𝐃𝐄𝐍𝐓𝐈𝐓𝐘 𝐁𝐑𝐈𝐃𝐆𝐄 — BRIDGE TO WEB3


The crypto wallet physically lives in the "Sovereign" enclave. When any app (DEX in "Human", e-commerce in "Citizen" accepting crypto) wants to sign a transaction — it sends a request through a system intent to "Sovereign".

"Sovereign" shows explicit confirmation with a full transaction breakdown (what is being signed, where the funds go, what is the amount, what are the fees). Only after explicit user confirmation, "Sovereign" signs the transaction inside its enclave and returns the signature.

The private key never leaves "Sovereign". This is integration with the SovereignBankWeb3 ecosystem at the architectural level.


       🧩  𝐕𝐀𝐔𝐋𝐓 𝐅𝐑𝐀𝐆𝐌𝐄𝐍𝐓𝐀𝐓𝐈𝐎𝐍 — KEY FRAGMENTATION


The access key to "Sovereign" is not stored in one place. It is mathematically divided into three fragments (Shamir's Secret Sharing):

   🔒  One — in the Android Keystore (Trusted Execution Environment of your processor)
   ☁️  One — in the Sovereign Sync cloud, encrypted with your password
   💍  One — optionally on a physical NFC carrier (token, card, ring)

Loss of one fragment is not a catastrophe. Theft of one is insufficient for entry. Loss of the phone — "Sovereign" is restored from the two remaining fragments on a new device.

Also, "Sovereign" will provide the option to use a simpler key, which will be stored only in your head.

"Sovereign" encryption uses post-quantum algorithms on top of standard AES — CRYSTALS-Kyber for key exchange, CRYSTALS-Dilithium for signatures (standardized by NIST in 2024).

Protection against the "store now, decrypt later" attack. All serious intelligence services in the world are currently collecting encrypted traffic and encrypted data into an archive, hoping to decrypt them in 10–15 years when quantum computers appear. Standard AES-256 encryption will not survive that era. Post-quantum algorithms will.

Sovereign is the only application in the world ready for the quantum era at this level.

We must be honest with the user.

There exists an Israeli software-hardware complex for extracting data from mobile devices, used by law enforcement agencies around the world. This is not a myth or a legend — it is a serious tool capable of extracting data from most phones in most scenarios.

This does not mean that Sovereign makes data extraction impossible. Any claim of "absolute protection" is a lie, and we do not make it. A state with physical access to the device and unlimited time will find ways. But Sovereign makes extraction many times more expensive, longer, and less productive:

   🛡️  When seizing a phone in a powered-off or forcibly rebooted state (triggers — prolonged inactivity, geo-fence, incorrect attempts, gesture) — the attacker holds an encrypted brick, not data. CE-keys of "Human" and "Sovereign" are not in memory. Extraction requires brute force through TEE with hardware rate-limiting — that is years of computation.

   🔐  On top of system file-based encryption — a second layer of own "Sovereign" encryption with an independent key. Even if an attacker somehow obtains the Work Profile CE-key, the decrypted "Sovereign" files inside are a second encrypted volume.

   🧬  Hardware-backed keys in TEE with automatic invalidation upon attempts to manipulate biometrics or screen lock policy.

   🌫️  Veil mode — the "Sovereign" container is cryptographically indistinguishable from random data, its existence cannot be proven without a key.

   🪤  Decoy-"Sovereign" and Forensic Bait — the attacker spends time on empty shells.

The Sovereign project does not call for illegal activity. Sovereign does not help criminals. Sovereign protects ordinary people from ordinary risks of ordinary life in an era when any data can be used against them in ten years in circumstances that do not exist today.
